Currently at Al Hamwi BioMedical Research Center.

Throughout our journey, which began in 2008, we have been able to innovate and develop several vital technologies, which have been globally accredited. The most prominent of these innovations are:

  • Therapy of Retinitis Pigmentosa: Treatment for this intractable disease, which has baffled scientists for decades, is now being developed through the Re-Sequence of Nitrogenous Bases in DNA, thereby correcting the genetic mutations in five human genes associated with the retina.
  • OCT-Based Disease Early Detection: Early detection of the most serious and complex diseases affecting humans, including cancerous and silent diseases. This is achieved by analyzing the OCT Retinal Scan in just 7 seconds and linking it to algorithms for Pathological Signs, thus detecting the disease before it develops.
  • Genetically Modified Ostrich Corneas: Utilizing the eye cornea of a rare South African Black Neck Ostrich breed, after genetically modifying this bird to be a replacement for a damaged human eye cornea.

This center was established in 2008. We utilize the latest therapeutic technologies globally, in addition to finding radical solutions for the most complex cases that have left scientists baffled for decades. Throughout our history, we have provided medical services to more than 30 countries worldwide.

Our medical research focuses on developing healthcare through innovation, advanced diagnostic methods, and a deeper understanding of the root causes of diseases. We seamlessly translate laboratory findings into practical applications within hospital operating rooms, surgical authorities, and clinics.

Additionally, we actively train and develop clinical researchers, professional surgical talents, and medical students.
