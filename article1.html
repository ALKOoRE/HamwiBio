Genetic therapy for Retinitis Pigmentosa Patients.

April 1 2025

Research leaded by: Dr Morhaf Al Hamwi / Ophthalmologist, Royal College of Ophthalmologists Member/ UK, director of "Al Hamwi BioMedical Research Centre".
In this centre, we are now conducting many Ophthalmic BioMedical research studies, the latest one is concerning Retinitis Pigmentosa cooperating with a highly qualified team of geneticists. This incurable disease made researchers baffled for decades. This research is considered as a scientific revolution in every sense of the word.
People with mutations in RHO, USH2A, RPGR, RP2 and RPE65 genes will benefit from this type of therapy.
As the above mentioned genetic mutations lead to:

  • Deaf-Blindness Usher Syndrome.

  • Autosomal Dominant & Recessive Retinitis Pigmentosa (AD-RP) & (AR-RP).

  • X-linked Retinitis Pigmentosa.

  • Defect in Retinal Pigment Epithelium (RPE).

We are looking forward finding the radical solution for all Retinitis Pigmentosa Patients as soon as possible.
